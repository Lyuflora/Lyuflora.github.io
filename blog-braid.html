Braid Game Analyse

A cool puzzle game

Thinking every directions possible, players will run into various fun facts in Braid's unique rules.

Braid is a platform and puzzle game. The game centers on the character of Tim who chasing the princess and try to rescue her. The player has multiple abilities of time manipulation, collecting pieces of puzzle in each world. Braid is full of symbols and metaphors like an allegory, such as "repent", "escape", "choice". The game is presented as an anthology of Tim's life through pure words narration, and wraps the story in the mechanics and puzzles of each world.

It is quite easy to reverse time and erase plays' mistakes in Braid, which allows players to mend their wrongdoings, at the same time, have a deeper understand of rules. In Braid's world, precision of every movement is less required. Instead, Braid encourages players to focus on methods of solving the puzzle. If the player has difficulty in achieving his goal, just use time controlling and get back to that puzzle again. Therefore, players solve puzzles with full concentration, correcting mistakes whenever they find a better method. For example, the player controls Tim to jump upon a higher platform, and the monster on the other side will be shot out of a cannon. Although landing on the monster's head is somewhat dangerous, the player can slightly adjust Tim's position in the air by reversing time.

In Braid, Tim's death does not interrupt the player's logic thinking, instead, it becomes a start of going back, which allows the player to see how the world changes, find clues and solutions. Such elements as "death" and "losing the game" have changed their meanings in Braid. When death occurs to Tim, the player will realize he/she need to do something to make it right. It is a part of the game, allowing the player to explore the way of solving the puzzle freely, and more importantly, observe and understand how different behaviors will affect the world. For example, in a level of World 4, the movement of all the monsters seems inexplicable, but the player can find out the logic behind after some trying at the cost of several times of death--the protagonist's moving direction determines the direction of time.
